Project Proposal

Description

The project proposal formally establishes the research scope for SnoutScan - a non-invasive, AI-powered biometric and welfare platform for dogs. It defines the research problem, outlines the four specific objectives (nose-print identification, lost-pet recovery, ethical breeding recommendation, and cruelty detection), and presents a high-level system architecture and initial literature review.

The proposal also maps each sub-objective to a team member, justifies the technology stack (Flutter, React/TypeScript, Node.js/Express, Python/FastAPI, PostgreSQL), and establishes the project timeline across the 2025/26 academic year under the supervision of Prof. Dasuni Nawinna.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Clarity and relevance of the research problem statement
  • Feasibility and novelty of the proposed solution
  • Quality and depth of the initial literature review
  • Realistic timeline, resource planning, and team allocation

Description

The final assessment is the culmination of the SnoutScan research project. It requires submission of the complete, production-ready system alongside a comprehensive research report. The report covers all four components - biometric identification, lost-pet recovery, breeding recommendation, and cruelty detection - with full methodology, model evaluation, results analysis, and conclusions.

The system submission includes the deployed Flutter mobile application, React back-office portal, Node.js/Express and FastAPI backend services, trained AI/ML models, and a live PostgreSQL-backed database environment. All components must pass integration testing and meet the stated non-functional requirements (privacy, scalability, accuracy targets).

Evaluation Criteria

  • Completeness and production-readiness of the full system
  • Research report quality, academic rigour, and depth of analysis
  • Achievement of the four stated research objectives and accuracy targets
  • Validity, reproducibility, and clarity of results and conclusions

Description

The viva is an individual oral examination where each group member defends their specific sub-objective before an academic panel. Members are assessed on their depth of understanding, the technical and research decisions they made, and their individual contribution to the overall SnoutScan platform.

Each member is expected to explain their AI/ML model choices, justify architectural decisions, discuss experimental results, and articulate how their component integrates into the unified welfare platform. The panel may probe any aspect of the research, implementation, or evaluation.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Individual depth of understanding of the research topic and their component
  • Ability to defend design, architecture, and implementation decisions
  • Clarity and accuracy of answers to panel questions
  • Demonstrated individual contribution to the overall project
